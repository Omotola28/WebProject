TWITTER CHANGES USER EXPERIENCE; 140-CHARACTER LIMIT UPDATED

  • 25 MAY 2016
  • SOCIAL MEDIA
  • TWITTER

Twitter is one of my favourite apps to share ideas and read other people’s content and in over a decade using the 140-character limit restriction has not always been a problem but rethinking ways in which one can communicate or reply effectively but still in a fun way has been somewhat daunting. There is great news though twitter announced that the 140-character limit would be more adequate by removing some characters from being counted when tweeting.

HERE IS WHAT HAS CHANGED

Replying Mentions

When replying to a tweet or mention the @names will no longer be included in the word count. This will make ongoing conversations more straight forward and easier, no more unnecessary shorthand for words to make sure conversations are relayed to the group effectively.

Media attachments

Adding photos, gifts, polls or even quotes will no longer count to the 140-character limit set. Leaving more room to type in tweets

Retweet and quote your tweets

Twitter will also be adding functionality that enables you retweet or quote yourself just in case a tweet needs to be re-emphasized, reflected on or wasn’t as popular as it should have been.

WHAT DO YOU THINK ABOUT THIS UPDATE?

In my own opinion, I would say it’s a great feature added to the user experience on twitter. Conversations would be easier, faster and more comprehensive. What other ideas do you think twitter should add? What are your own opinions do you wish they got rid of the character limit entirely?

WHAT YOU SHOULD KNOW ABOUT DOMAIN AUTHORITY(DA) & HOW TO CHECK IT

  • 25 MAY 2016
  • SOCIAL MEDIA
  • TWITTER

First off let’s define what domain authority is. Domain authority is a score on a 100-point scale that was built by Moz to predict how well a website will do in search engines. It is important to know this because companies are often interested in your DA score when want to work with you and if in comparison to another website your score is not as high that wouldn’t be good to improve your income stats.

It’s also important to know that Domain Authority is worth measuring against other bloggers or “competitive” sites rather than as a score on it’s own or against your SEO efforts. It builds up very slowly over many years, so don’t feel hard done by if your score isn’t growing. Not even Moz itself have 100/100 DA Score. A DA of 30+ will start showing some authority in search engines. Domain Authority is ONLY for custom domains! It does not work for subdomains – such as .blogspot.com or .wordpress.com. If your blog uses one of these, then it’s a subdomain of that platform. The DA score you are getting is for the main domain (blogspot.com or wordpress.com) and does not reflect your blogs score.

HOW IS DA SCORE CALCULATED?

Moz score Domain authority based on a 100-point logarithmic scale. Therefore, it is easier to grow your score form 20 – 30 than it is from 70-80. Therefore, what factors affect this score.

1.LONGEVITY OF WEBSITE

The amount of time that a website has been functioning indicated its relevance and concludes that it’s a trusted source of information and not a spam website. The amount of traffic drawn and improvement in the SEO of the website also plays a role.

2. HOW POPULAR IS YOUR WEBSITE IS?

Popularity can be measured differently maybe by the number of shares from post or inbound links from other sites to your website just shows that it is a relevant website. Therefore, enhancing the DA score

3.SIZE OF WEBSITE

The size of the website indicates that there is more information and more links on the page to get to. A larger site with better content will normally have more links that a smaller site.

4. OTHER

This could include factors like Moz Rank, Moz Trust, link profile etc. these can impact the score. There is no particular method that is used as there are multiple conditions that are considered before the DA Score is increased.

HOW TO CHECK YOUR SCORE

You can go to Moz.com and type in your url, only root domain names are calculated as domains with attachments like blogspot.com or WordPress .com are considered subdomains.
